# 5. Discussion

The experiments confirm three interlocking claims. ISO 12913 perceptual descriptors provide structured generative conditioning value beyond raw audio embeddings, yielding a city-level FID of 24.1 that beats both Sound2Scene (28.3; Sung-Bin et al., 2023) and the single-path acoustic baseline (Arm C, FID 26.7). The dual-path architecture that fuses local OSM spatial controls with global soundscape embeddings delivers further and statistically reliable improvement. And geographic context modulates generation fidelity in a spatially non-stationary manner that requires MGWR to capture: residual Moran's I falls from a pre-regression value of approximately 0.26 to 0.07 after MGWR adjustment, confirming that acoustic and morphological drivers operate at fundamentally different spatial scales. The following subsections interpret each result against prior work, discuss limitations, and identify practical implications.

---

## 5.1 Interpretation of Generation Fidelity Gains

**Link to RQ1:** Can urban soundscape features serve as effective conditioning signals for street-level image synthesis?

The 9.7% FID gain of the full dual-path model (Arm D, FID 24.1) over the best single-path precursor (Arm C, FID 26.7) — with non-overlapping bootstrap 95% confidence intervals ([23.0, 25.3] vs. [25.4, 28.1]) — is directly attributable to the ISO 12913 conditioning head. The ablation isolates this contribution cleanly: Arm C includes CLAP embeddings and OSM spatial controls but lacks the ISO 12913 MLP head that maps CLAP features to pleasantness, eventfulness, and LAeq scores. The performance gap therefore reflects the informational content carried by structured perceptual descriptors beyond what a raw 512-dimensional CLAP embedding encodes.

This result is consistent with the cross-modal theory established by Kang et al. (2018), who demonstrated in a three-city field study that soundscape pleasantness and eventfulness co-vary systematically with visual scene character — with calm, low-eventfulness soundscapes predicting greener, lower-density visual environments and high-eventfulness, low-pleasantness soundscapes predicting commercial and transit scenes. Pleasantness and eventfulness are precisely calibrated to this visual-acoustic coupling: a high-pleasantness soundscape signal carries an implicit scene-category prior (residential or park) that a raw CLAP embedding, trained on general environmental sounds across LAION-Audio-630K, does not. The +0.06 mIoU gain from Arm C to Arm D on semantic segmentation confirms that this prior propagates to improved scene content alignment, not merely distributional statistics.

The city-level average FID of 24.1 positions SoundScape-ControlNet favorably relative to both the external sound-to-image baseline Sound2Scene (Sung-Bin et al., 2023; FID 28.3 on VEGAS) and the street-level synthesis benchmark Toker et al. (2021; FID ~30 on CVUSA/CVACT). It is important to acknowledge that these comparisons are not strictly controlled: VEGAS is a general sound dataset rather than a geotagged urban soundscape collection, and Toker et al. operate in a satellite-to-street cross-view synthesis regime rather than an acoustic conditioning regime. FID is also known to be sensitive to reference set size and feature extractor choice (Heusel et al., 2017); the present evaluation uses a held-out Mapillary test set of 500 images per city with Inception v3 activations, which is consistent but not identical to the Sound2Scene evaluation protocol. The comparison therefore supports the directional claim — SoundScape-ControlNet is competitive with or better than the prior state of the art on generation fidelity — while recognizing that a controlled head-to-head evaluation on identical test sets remains methodologically desirable.

The weaker ISO 12913 contribution in Singapore relative to NYC and London — reflected in the smallest Wilcoxon p-value for that city (p = 0.0031, still below the Bonferroni threshold) and the relatively modest zero-shot LPIPS advantage — is consistent with the ARAUS training distribution, which overrepresents Northern European and North American urban soundscapes (Ooi et al., 2022). Singapore's tropical compact morphology produces acoustic signatures less well represented in ARAUS, meaning the ISO 12913 MLP head generalizes with reduced precision to Singapore — a limitation we address in Section 5.3.

## 5.2 Spatial Heterogeneity and MGWR Insights

**Link to RQ3:** How does geographic context spatially modulate generation quality, and can GWR/MGWR capture these spatially varying relationships?

The MGWR analysis delivers the most interpretively rich findings of the study. The pre-regression spatial autocorrelation of per-cell median LPIPS (Moran's I averaging approximately 0.26 across the three cities; NYC 0.28, London 0.25, Singapore 0.22) confirms that generation fidelity is not spatially random: high-error cells cluster in commercial and transport cores while low-error cells cluster in residential and vegetated zones. This spatial structure indicates that a single global model cannot adequately characterize how acoustic and morphological covariates relate to generation quality, and it motivates the GWR/MGWR progression.

MGWR outperforms GWR and OLS on every fit criterion in every city. The AICc improvements of GWR over OLS (NYC 153, London 122, Singapore 104 units) already demonstrate substantial spatial non-stationarity; the additional MGWR gains over GWR (NYC 36, London 30, Singapore 17 units) reveal that per-variable bandwidth calibration provides explanatory value beyond a single global neighborhood size. The key insight lies in the bandwidth structure reported in Table 4. Acoustic pleasantness operates at the most local spatial scale (mean k = 55, ~27.5 km effective diameter, bootstrap 95% CI [48, 63]), while building density operates at a distinctly broader regional scale (mean k = 140, ~70 km effective diameter, bootstrap 95% CI [128, 153]). These confidence intervals do not overlap, confirming statistically distinguishable spatial scales of operation at p < 0.05.

This scale separation reflects acoustic physics. Soundscape pleasantness — a perceptual aggregate of spectral content, temporal variation, and source composition — varies at the block to sub-neighborhood scale, consistent with the block-level variation in ISO 12913 pleasantness documented by Watcharasupat et al. (2022) and with the finding by Qin et al. (2019) that acoustic-visual Pearson correlations of 0.52–0.74 are measured at the land-use zone level rather than at broader scales. Building density, by contrast, defines morphological zones (high-rise commercial cores, medium-density residential belts, low-density suburbs) that extend over neighborhoods and districts — a spatial scale appropriately captured by the regional bandwidth.

The pleasantness coefficient exhibits a spatially heterogeneous pattern that aligns with urban structure. In residential zones and urban parks, the pleasantness coefficient is positive and significant: higher pleasantness predicts lower LPIPS (better generation quality), consistent with acoustically calmer environments producing stronger acoustic-visual coherence. In major transport interchange zones — Midtown and the Financial District in NYC, the City of London and Canary Wharf in London, the CBD and Jurong Industrial Estate in Singapore — the pleasantness coefficient weakens toward zero or reverses sign, indicating that acoustic pleasantness no longer reliably predicts which visual scene will appear. This reversal is consistent with Qin et al. (2019), who found weaker acoustic-visual correspondence (r ~0.30) in mixed-use and commercial zones compared to residential and park contexts (r = 0.52–0.74). In acoustically complex commercial environments, multiple competing sound sources (traffic, pedestrian activity, mechanical systems) reduce the discriminative capacity of a single pleasantness score as a scene-category prior.

The time-of-day bandwidth (mean k = 220, ~110 km, quasi-global) reveals a structurally different pattern: diurnal acoustic variation imposes a spatially stationary effect on generation fidelity within each city. The acoustic character of morning rush hours versus midday versus evening is consistent enough across urban zones that a city-wide bandwidth adequately captures the time-of-day effect. This finding validates the decision to include time-of-day as a conditioning input: its effect is real (the bandwidth is well-identified and distinguishable from noise) but spatially undifferentiated.

Fan et al. (2020) achieved residual Moran's I of 0.08 after GWR for London soundscape-land-use data — a benchmark that MGWR matches or exceeds in all three cities of the present study (NYC 0.07, London 0.06, Singapore 0.08). The reduction from OLS residual Moran's I values of 0.21–0.28 to MGWR residual values of 0.06–0.08 confirms that the MGWR specification captures the spatial structure in generation fidelity without over-fitting: the residuals are near-spatially-independent (pseudo-p > 0.05 in London and NYC under MGWR), while a spatially structured error term that could mask model misspecification is absent. The parallel reduction documented by Shen et al. (2022) — from OLS Moran's I ~0.23 to GWR ~0.06 for urban morphology and noise modeling — further confirms that this reduction pattern is a reproducible signature of well-specified spatially varying models on urban acoustic-morphological data.

## 5.3 Cross-City Generalizability and Limitations

**Link to RQ2 and RQ3:** Which spatial inputs improve generalizability, and how does geographic context modulate generation quality?

Zero-shot transfer from NYC + London to Singapore yields a 13.3% FID degradation for the dual-path model (FID 24.1 to 27.3), staying below the pre-specified 15% threshold and below the FID 30 usability ceiling. The acoustic-only model (Arm C) degrades by 32.3% (FID 26.7 to 35.3) — a 19.0 percentage-point gap that quantifies the contribution of OSM spatial conditioning to cross-city portability. OSM land-use categories, road hierarchy, and building density surfaces are structurally consistent across cities in a way that CLAP and ISO 12913 embeddings calibrated to NYC and London soundscapes are not. This result replicates the finding of Mai et al. (2023), who demonstrated zero-shot transfer of a purely spatial OSM-conditioned diffusion model from NYC to London and Tokyo; the present study extends this evidence to acoustic-visual conditioning and establishes that spatial conditioning is the portability mechanism even when acoustic conditioning is present.

Practically, the 13.3% FID degradation for Singapore means that the model generates Singapore scenes with somewhat reduced fidelity — generated images are still statistically plausible (FID 27.3 < 30) but exhibit modestly reduced per-image sharpness and acoustic-visual coherence (LPIPS 0.31 vs. in-sample 0.28). The spatial pattern of this degradation, concentrated in the Marina Bay financial core and Orchard Road commercial corridor, indicates that the degradation is not uniform: acoustically and morphologically complex zones that already exhibited high-error characteristics in-sample are disproportionately affected, consistent with the MGWR band structure in which locally-varying acoustic pleasantness drives error in these zones.

Four limitations constrain the scope of these conclusions. First, despite the 3-year temporal exclusion criterion, a residual temporal mismatch persists between acoustic measurement dates and Mapillary image capture dates. A fully temporally matched dataset — requiring co-located, simultaneous audio-image collection — would eliminate this confound but is not achievable with currently available open data sources. Second, the Noise-Planet crowdsourced acoustic data used for LAeq kriging exhibits a density bias toward central urban areas: peripheral and industrial zones have sparser acoustic measurement coverage, and kriging interpolation errors in these areas are larger. MGWR results in low-measurement-density zones should therefore be interpreted with greater caution than results in acoustically well-sampled central areas. Third, MGWR assumes that the relationship between each covariate and the dependent variable is continuous and smooth across space at the covariate's optimal bandwidth scale; the model cannot capture abrupt transitions at zone boundaries (e.g., a hard edge between a park and a motorway interchange) without cell-level discontinuity. Fourth, Mapillary image density is uneven across cities and across land-use zones within cities: commercial cores and tourist corridors are overrepresented while industrial and peripheral zones are underrepresented, introducing potential selection bias in the training and evaluation distributions.

## 5.4 Implications for Urban Planning and GeoAI

**Link to RQ1 and RQ2:** Does soundscape conditioning enable practical downstream urban applications?

SoundScape-ControlNet addresses three practical urban planning needs. The framework's demonstrated zero-shot usability in Singapore (FID 27.3 < 30) establishes acoustic gap-filling as a feasible application: in cities where street-level image coverage is sparse but acoustic and OSM data are available — as is the case across much of the Global South via Noise-Planet and OSM — the model synthesizes plausible scene imagery from acoustic + spatial inputs without requiring local image training data. This directly supports objective 5 of the research program (acoustic-based gap-filling for data-sparse cities), identified as gap G3 in the synthesis. For soundscape intervention design, the MGWR coefficient maps identify the specific zones where modifying acoustic pleasantness — through noise barriers, traffic calming, or vegetation — would most improve the visual coherence of generated scenes, providing planners with a spatial prioritization map for acoustic interventions. Finally, because the framework generates synthetic imagery rather than retrieving or distributing captured street-level photographs, it offers a privacy-preserving alternative to Mapillary or Street View for applications where photographic coverage of residential areas is restricted by data-protection regulation.
